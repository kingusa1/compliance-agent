Compliance Training Manual
1. Purpose of This Manual
This manual provides mandatory compliance training for all staff involved in sales,
administration, pricing, or customer contact. Its purpose is to ensure all activity complies
with UK regulatory expectations, supplier rules, and internal standards, with a strong focus
on complaint prevention and customer transparency.
Compliance is not optional. Failure to follow these standards can result in complaints,
supplier disputes, commission clawbacks, regulatory action, and reputational damage.

2. Regulatory Framework
2.1 Ofgem Standards of Conduct
All customer interactions must be:
Honest, transparent, and fair
Clear and not misleading
Free from pressure or coercion
Accurately documented
Key principles:
Customers must understand who we are, what we do,
and what they are agreeing to.
Prices, commissions, and contract terms must be clear
No information may be hidden, rushed, or
misrepresented2.2 Broker Responsibilities
As a Third-Party Intermediary (TPI), we must:
Clearly identify ourselves as a broker
Explain our role vs the supplier
Disclose commission and services
Act in the customer’s best interests

4.1 Commission Disclosure Rules
Commission must be disclosed clearly and understandably
Disclosure must not be rushed or hidden at the end of a call
Customers must be told:
That commission is included in the rate
The total commission amount or p/kWh equivalent
4.2 Price Explanations
Price increases must not be blamed solely on “market conditions” if commission is included
Any savings or increases must be explained honestly
Comparisons must be accurate and provableWatt Sales Partner Guide V.1

5. Sales Call Compliance
5.1 Pre-Sales Calls
Must include:
Customer authority and decision-maker confirmation
Clear explanation of the purpose of the call
No pricing commitment or pressure
Prohibited Behaviours
Pressure selling
Misleading statements
Implying contracts are mandatory
Rushing confirmation scripts5.2 Sales Calls
Must demonstrate:
Clear offer explanation
Contract length and start date
Rates and standing charges
Commission disclosure
Opportunity for customer questions

6. Verbal Recording Audits
All calls are subject to audit.
Compliance Officers will check for:
Clear disclosures
Accurate information
Proper customer consent
Professional conduct
Any failed audit may result in retraining, contract amendment or cancellation and in serious
cases, escalation to management.

8. Confirmation Procedures
Purpose:
To ensure the customer fully understands and agrees to the contract and to ensure
compliance. Confirmation calls or emails after the sale is completed to ensure we
have taken appropriate steps to confirm who we are, the agreed terms of contract and
we have been transparent should a complaint arise. 
Mandatory Steps:
A written confirmation email is sent after all contracts confirming:
Contract summary provided
Terms and conditions disclosed 
Confirmation logged on CRM
